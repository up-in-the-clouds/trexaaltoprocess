Companies & NGOs

This section is specifically designed for companies, NGOs and other Civil Society Organizations to learn about the clothing value chain, post-consumer clothing management strategies and challenges, and designing materials to enhance circular economy in the clothing sector. The learning tools include videos, interviews, studies, and an interactive toolkit to explain circular economy and waste hierarchy in this sector. Our aim is to equip you with the knowledge and tools necessary to understand, navigate, and actively contribute to the sustainable transformation of the clothing industry.

Design Guidelines

The design guidelines for chemical recycling are being created with the aim of informing and guiding apparel designers and product developers on the design of recyclable products, in a comprehensive, realistic and impactful way, considering what is techno-economically feasible today. It guides the design of garments primarily made from polyester, polyamide 6 and cotton, working with the product’s use-case as the starting point. Information on the wider context of sustainable fashion design strategies is also provided. The guidelines will be published at the end of the project, in May 2025.

Redesign Case

The Redesign case is an initiative of Aalto University, taking place during summer 2024 in collaboration with Finnish second-hand clothing platform Emmy. A group of eight MA Fashion students from Aalto University are redesigning and remaking four – eight garments into capsule collections using Emmy’s unsold stock. These will then be available for sale on the Emmy online store. Alongside this upcycling exercise, a comparative object analysis will be conducted on the garments in their original reused vs. upcycled states.

Collecting, Sorting, Recycling

This section looks at what happens to textiles when they're no longer needed or wanted. We'll see how they are collected, get sorted, who handles them, and what can be done with them afterward. Through easy-to-understand videos, we'll learn about the steps involved in managing textile waste properly. We'll also explain why it's important to dispose of textiles responsibly and how everyone can help make a difference.

Citizen Engagement Activities Toolkit

After planning different types of citizen engagement activities, we are building a toolkit for companies or NGOs in which we include our learnings and best practices for planning successful citizen engagement activities that encourage more sustainable and circular consumption.

Consumers

This section presents different scientific research on the topic of textile and clothing consumption behavior, to help understand consumers' motivations and barriers to adopting more circular consumption practices.

Video Library of Learnings

This section presents the lessons learnt in the undertaking of the Textile Recycling Excellence project. It includes videos and interviews with members of the consortium, where they discuss the challenges of collection, sorting, and recycling of post-consumer clothes. Additionally, the role of performing techno-economic assessment, and social and environmental life cycle assessments of the textile value chain developed for this sector under the T-REX project is examined.
